We remember a story, if I recall correctly it starts as they all do.

Once upon a time there was a boy [which, as we all know, is the same as a man, but with entirely too much energy and a conspicuous lack of wisdom]. He was bright and full of potential, as they all are if you bother to ask them. Anyway, this boy has a name, but we’ve so utterly forgotten it, even his mother couldn’t help us. The liberty is then mine to refer to him as Icarus, solely because I think that's a dope-ass name, even though he would never respond to that title.

Icarus was out for a walk, as one does, and passed by a snake in a fez, as they do, who happened to be selling some sort of oily seeds. We must take a moment to acknowledge the Germanic insistence that these were magical bean seeds [however distastefully passé that might be]. It would be much better had they been apple seed, or seeds of doubt, or invitations to a ball (which tend to be some of the best seeds). If I were the author, I’d say they were grape seeds, since grapes need a support to grow, but I’m not the author and the type of seed is not recorded. Icarus figured he should be eating seeds as a pastime activity, and went in for the sale. A passing goose in a fez suggested he not so alter his life, but the snake in a fez offered a compelling deal, so the transaction was verified after 24-48 hours of processing. With his rather delightful seeds in hand, Icarus immediately forgets his hopes and dreams, eats 2, finds that they taste terrible, and concludes such seeds are much better off planted beside a secluded spring guarded by the elemental forces of nature.

With the passage of time, Icarus develops into a youth, now better known as Prince Charming. One day, the Prince finds himself wandering in the woods where he had once spent his childhood, and stumbles on a most exotic collection of biological growths commonly referred to as plants. Now, given his youth and proclivities, he would have certainly passed by, had that growth not reminded him of some aesthetic arrangement of feminine features. On closer inspection, Charming finds a grove of five entwinned trees of immense girth, incalculable height, and bearing more fruit per branch (fpb) then any prior tree had yet achieved.
